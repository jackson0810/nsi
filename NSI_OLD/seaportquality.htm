  | Quality Assurance |

NSI’s primary guarantor of service quality is uncompromising standards of client service, reinforced daily through company communications and by the example of its leadership. Other tools of quality assurance are well understood by NSI management and employed effectively. These tools include project management software and corporate calendars with key milestones and deliverables due dates. Status of major company deliverables are reviewed at weekly team meetings and smaller task deliverable status is monitored by respective task managers. This QA process is facilitated via monthly reports on project status to ensure on-time and on-cost delivery. Routine, repetitive work is “mistake proofed” with written step-by-step procedures to build quality into the product and minimize rework.

Perhaps most important to NSI quality assurance in an environment where service requirements and product features are rapidly evolving is a policy and culture of open communications. This kind of operating environment is fostered through extemporaneous project teams – sometimes meeting in physical proximity and sometimes joining up on the Ethernet – formed to attack a specific problem and as quickly disbanding, then forming with a slightly different composition to attack a different problem. This is a non-traditional approach to quality assurance suitable when innovation and adaptation emerge as the most critical features in defining quality.
