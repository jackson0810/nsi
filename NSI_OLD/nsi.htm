NSI is an aggressive, fast-growing, dynamic business delivering superior
defense acquisition and commercial professional services throughout our Navy
Marine Corps Team. Leadership within NSI brings forward thinking subject
matter experts throughout all disciplines within defense acquisition
management including program management, systems engineering, logistics
engineering, test and evaluation, information technology, financial management
and audit. Currently supporting NAVAIR EA-6B, H-1, Aircrew Systems and
Network-Centric Warfare programs as well as the Research and Engineering,
Logistics, Comptroller, and Business/Financial Management competencies,
NSI brings critical skills and is efficiently delivering solutions leading to the
emergence of transformational capabilities demanded by DoD. NSI is veteran
owned small business.		  

August 2007 LOGCAP IV Support – NSI was awarded a subcontract with SERCO, Inc, in support of a Logistics Civil Augmentation Planning Contract (LOGCAP IV).  Under this contract, NSI will provide logistics acquisition and life cycle management support.  Under our first Task Assignment Record (TAR 001), issued earlier this month, NSI will provide technical and audit services assistance related to property book management and accountability and technical and financial services assistance, with employees working in IRAQ.  This award represents our first in-theater support contract.

August 2007 CEOSS Support – NSI has been selected to the SERCO Commercial Enterprise Omnibus Support Services (CEOSS) team. The SERCO team will support the Business and Analytical Domain under the CEOSS program.  The CEOSS contract provides business and analytical contractor support service to the Marine Corps Systems Command.

March 1, 2007 (China Lake, CA) – NSI was awarded a contract to provide maintenance and operations oversight of OPNAVINST 4790 Unmanned Air Vehicles assigned to the UAS Technical Project Office (TPO) and Weapons System Support Activity (WSSA) organization(s) at China Lake, CA. NSI will provide subject matter and technical expertise on Unmanned Air Systems (UAS) operations and maintenance to support the integration of unmanned weapon systems into the Tactical Air Combat Training Systems (TACTS).

Feb 1, 2007 (Jacksonville, FL) – NSI was awarded a follow-on FFP contract to provide Performance Based Logistics Support and Supply Chain Management services to prime contractor AAI in partnership with NADEP Jacksonville.

Jan 11, 2007 (Patuxent River, MD) – NSI was awarded a SeaPort-e Deliver Order (N00178-05-D-4466, M804) to provide Logistics Support to In-Service VH-3D and VH-60N Presidential Helicopter assigned to Marine Helicopter Squadron One (HMX-1). Under this delivery order, NSI will support the In-Service VH APML/PSTL and preserve the availability, maintainability and supportability of weapons systems, subsystems and support systems currently in place. The total value of this contract is $3,992,234.

31 July 2006 (Patuxent River, MD) – NSI was awarded a SeaPort-e Deliver Order for EA-6B Special Tooling, Configuration Management, and ECP programs under Contract No. N00178-05-D-4466, valued at $941,380 base year and $4,989,105 total, including four option years.  This is NSI’s first prime contract award under the SeaPort-e contract.  NSI is a small veteran owned business specializing in engineering, logistics, management consulting, and technical analysis for Naval Systems.  NSI’s current business in focused on maximizing the cost-effectiveness of Naval Aviation to the Navy/Marine Corps Team in a joint and coalition effects-based combat environment.  NSI’s business operations are concentrated at Patuxent River, MD with a team also operating out of Jacksonville, FL and personnel located near Cherry Point, NC. 

Dec. 1, 2006 (Patuxent River, MD) - NSI was awarded a SeaPort-e Deliver Order for Logistics Program Management Support and Logistics Supportability Analyses for the E-2/C-2 Weapon System, Power and Propulsion System, and Support Equipment under Contract No. N00178-05-D-4466. The total value of this contract $4,763,999. This is NSI's second prime contract awarded under our SeaPort-e contract.
