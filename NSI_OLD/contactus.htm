April 2008

NSI Opens Ridgecrest, CA Office

NSI announces the opening of our newest office in Ridgecrest, CA.  Our newest office will allow NSI personnel to provide a full range of subject matter expertise and technical services to the Naval Air Weapons Station, China Lake and surrounding community.  This is NSI’s third office and follows the recent opening of our second office in Jacksonville, FL.   Our Jacksonville, FL opened in the Fall of 2007.

                              

March 2008

NSI to provide Logistics, Maintenance and Supply Support (LMSS) to the DoD

As a subcontractor to Booz, Allen and Hamilton, NSI will provide Logistics, Maintenance and Supply Support (LMSS) to DoD agencies including the Navy, Air Force and Marine Corps.  This win will enable NSI to provide support to the Naval Air Systems Command (NAVAIR) Joint DoD Maintenance Resource Planning (MRP II) Program Office, PMA 203.  This contract supports the total life cycle systems management for business process services and solutions for logistics, maintenance, and supply functions and associated technologies. These services/solutions include all life cycle management activities associated with the planning, implementation, execution, and retirement of business processes and systems solutions for DoD Logistics, Maintenance and Supply Support activities and associated business initiatives, as well as those activities required for moving to an enterprise-wide, life cycle alignment of resources and outputs to achieve top-down performance driven outcomes

 

March 2008

NSI Awarded Enterprise Architecture Contract (Patuxent River, MD)

NSI was awarded a contract to assist NAVAIR PMA 263 with the development of an Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS) Family of Systems (FoS) enterprise architecture framework.  NSI will define an overarching FoS architecture that establishes a baseline to which the Naval NAS programs can migrate as required.   NSI will also be responsible for developing a UAS FoS architecture strategy that guides the Common Systems Integration IPT and UAS program in implementing the FoS architecture.  Ultimately, the UAS FoS enterprise architecture will give leadership the tool to measure commonality, interoperability, and the degree to which they have an “open” architecture framework.

 

May 2008

NSI participates in memorial Golf Tournament

NSI personnel participated in the 1st Annual Claude D. Alexander Memorial Golf Tournament.  Proceeds from this tournament were donated to the Injured Marine Semper Fi Fund.  This fund provides financial assistance to Marines injured in combat or in training to other service members

 

Feb 2008

NSI awarded Option for LOGCAP IV Support

NSI was awarded a follow-on effort to provide logistics acquisition and life cycle management support in support of in-theater efforts in IRAQ.  NSI will continue to successfully provide technical and audit services assistance related to property book management and accountability and technical and financial services assistance in support of in-theater efforts.

 

Feb 2008

NSI Awarded PMA 202 Metrics Support Contract (Patuxent River, MD)

NSI was awarded a second contract to provide performance and supply Metrics support to PMA202 Aircraft, Aircrew and ChemBio Systems products.  NSI will develop Metrics process procedures, extract and research data, and provide expert analyses and solutions to resolve outstanding PMA202 Performance and Supply Metrics issues on all PMA 202 managed products.

 

Feb 2008

AAI award Jacksonville Performance Based Logistics Support (PBL) Contract

NSI was award a follow-on contract to provide PBL and supply chain management services to prime contractor AAI in partnership with NADEP Jacksonville.  NSI will continue to provide logistics support services that will benefit the EA-6B aircraft, the operation and integration of the fleet, NADEP Jacksonville, and PMA 234.  Under this effort, NSI will continue to provide logistics element support and coordinate closely with NAVAIR/NADEP Jacksonville and NAVICP.

 

Jan 2008

NSI Awarded Moog-JAX Contract for Centralized Inventory Support for F/A-18C/D Leading Edge Flap

NSI was awarded a contract to perform a full range of centralized inventory support services to support the MOOG F/A-18 C/D Leading Edge Flap (LEF) Private Partnership at NADEP Jacksonville.  Under this effort, NSI functions as the single liaison for NADEP and MOOG ensuring successful coordination of repair efforts for the F/A-18 LEF transmission.  This coordination ensures timely receipt, inventory accuracy, handling efficiency, and secured storage for all parts received under this program and provides the inventory tools necessary for cost and schedule tracking of transmissions and the spare/repair parts inventory.
