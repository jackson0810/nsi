FMS (Foreign Military Sales) Mid Program Analyst

Duties and Responsibilities: Performs FMS acquisition, logistics, and program management throughout the life of FMS Case from implementation to closure. Performs analysis, gathers data and provides programmatic recommendations related to major weapons system in response to international customers’ requests. Assist in the preparation of agenda/minutes and monitoring actions related to various international customer meetings and program reviews. Obtains, gathers and analyzes cost estimates and delivery information to develop and transmit Price and Availability (P&A) and Letters of Offer and Acceptance (LOAs) (including amendments and modifications) to Navy International Programs Office, utilizing the Defense Security Assistance Management System (DSAMS).

Qualifications: Bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or university, or four (4) years of additional experience (to that cited below) relating to FMS acquisition program management may be substituted for the Bachelor’s degree. Requires a minimum of 4 (four) years of recent professional experience in Foreign Military Sales (FMS) case/program management. Must have knowledge and demonstrated understanding of policies governing Security Assistance established in the Arms Export Control Act (AECA) and the Foreign Assistance Act (FAA) and the Security Assistance Management Manual (SAMM, DOD 5105.38-M).

Clearance: Secret Desired

FMS (Foreign Military Sales) Junior Financial Analyst

Duties and Responsibilities: Assists the lead financial analyst in FMS case financial management from implementation to case closure. Tracks and monitors commitments, obligations, expenditures and deliveries to ensure accuracy of both the NAVAIR Program Office records and the Management Information System for International Logistics (MISIL). Responsibilities include gathering and assisting in data analysis of MISIL financial records and proposing resolutions/recommendations for corrective actions to discrepancies. Prepares financial documentation within the Navy ERP.

Qualifications: This position requires an Associate’s degree or 60+ hours toward a Bachelor’s degree from an accredited college or university or two (2) years of additional experience relating to FMS acquisition program management may be substituted for the Associates degree.
